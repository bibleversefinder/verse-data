1 Corinthians 15:43
"it is sown in dishonor, it is raised in glory; it is sown in weakness, it is raised in power;"
Context: Written around AD 55, 1 Corinthians addresses issues in the early church, including doubts about resurrection. Paul emphasizes the transformative power of Christ's resurrection, reassuring believers that physical death leads to a glorious, powerful new existence.
Reflection: In today's life, this passage encourages individuals facing challenges, failures, or weaknesses to find hope in the promise of renewal and strength through faith. It serves as a reminder that struggles can lead to personal and spiritual growth. The key theme is the contrast between earthly mortality and the glorious, powerful resurrection promised to believers.
