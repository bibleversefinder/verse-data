Ezra 4:3
"But Zerubbabel, Jeshua and the rest of the heads of the families of Israel answered, "You have no part with us in building a temple to our God. We alone will build it for the LORD, the God of Israel, as King Cyrus, the king of Persia, commanded us.""
Context: Ezra 4:3 is set in the post-exilic period, around 538 BC, when the Israelites were returning to Jerusalem after Babylonian captivity. This verse highlights the resistance faced by the Jewish leaders, Zerubbabel and Jeshua, as they sought to rebuild the temple, emphasizing their commitment to God's command amidst external opposition.
Reflection: In today's life, Ezra 4:3 teaches the importance of discernment and unity in pursuing God's purposes. It encourages individuals and communities to stand firm in their faith and mission, recognizing that not everyone shares their vision or commitment.
